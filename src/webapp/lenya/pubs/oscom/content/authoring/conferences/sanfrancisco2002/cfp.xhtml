Call for Participation for The Second Open Source Content Management Conference to be held in San Francisco from 25 to 27 September 2002.

The First Open Source Content Management Conference held on 21 and 22 March 2002 in Zurich was a great success. More than a hundred people attended.
There was much interest from people in the US to attend the conference, but Zurich was considered as being a bit too far away. Therefore, we decided to hold a second conference this year in the US.

For this second conference we are now calling on qualified speakers to participate.

It would be great if YOU could give a speech with regard to Open Source Content Management.

Please let us know as soon as possible at elisabeth.balzer@oscom.org. Deadline is set for 14 June 2002.

Very Preliminary Agenda

  • Keynote speaker
  • 1st and 2nd Day: one-track conference sessions (see also agenda of first conference )
  • 3rd Day: Workshops

The conference target audience are mainly developers of open-source content management software, frameworks, standards, etc.
All other interested parties are very welcome as well.
We set an upper limit of 200 attendees.

(up)

 

Topics for presentations and Format of sessions:

  • Conference Sessions:
     The first and second day of the conference will consist of one-track presentations which will normally last between 30 and 50 minutes.
    These presentations should be related to some angle of open-source content management. Potential areas of interest include Content Management Softwares / Frameworks / Editors / Standards (e.g. XACML, Webdav).
    Please see also the agenda of the first conference as reference.
    Papers will be evaluated on the basis of originality, significance of the contribution to the field and contribution to conference program diversity.
    Presentations given at the second conference will be posted during or just after the conference on the oscom.org site.

  • Workshops:
     The third day of the conference would be reserved for workshops. These workshops will give developers of open source content management software the possibility to explain and discuss specifics of their software with participants in a more direct environment.
    These workshops might be multi-track with either 2-hour- or half-day sessions.

  • Poster presentations:
     We are considering the possibility of Poster Presentations. They would be viewing only and remain throughout the conference.
